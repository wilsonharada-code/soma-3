For Organizations

A complementary visitor layer for Alberta's tourism ecosystem.

SomaVio helps visitors navigate Canmore and Kananaskis more safely and meaningfully — surfacing official guidance, local context and curated recommendations in one place. We're in early validation. This document explains what we're building and why it may be relevant to your work.

What SomaVio is

A digital companion for visitors already in the region — not a booking platform, not an OTA. A contextual layer built to complement what already exists.

Official safety, always visible

Trail conditions, wildlife alerts and weather from Parks Canada, Alberta Parks and Travel Alberta are always present — attributed, current and accessible in the visitor's language. SomaVio does not generate safety content. It surfaces and contextualizes official sources.

Local discovery, contextually

Recommendations adapt to real conditions — weather, time of day, trail status and visitor intent. A rainy afternoon surfaces different suggestions than a clear morning. Visitors get what's actually good right now, not a generic list.

Aggregated regional signals

As the platform scales, aggregated visitor behaviour data can help tourism organizations understand what visitors are actually doing — by category, time of day and context. Anonymized. Never individual. In pilot validation now.

Aligned with Alberta's tourism direction

Alberta's Provincial Tourism Strategy targets CAD $25B in annual tourism revenue by 2035 — a 60% increase from current levels. Achieving that goal requires not just more visitors, but better visitor experiences that generate repeat visits, word-of-mouth and deeper regional spending.

SomaVio addresses the in-destination experience gap: the moment between arrival and departure when visitors need local guidance, safety information and help making the most of where they are.

We're not a marketing platform. We don't attract visitors to Alberta. We help the visitors already here have a safer, richer, more locally connected experience — which is where yield improvement actually happens.

International arrivals in Alberta grew 4% in early 2026, against a national decline of 5%. That divergence reflects real regional strength — and real visitor demand for meaningful experience, not just access.

The gap we're addressing

Three real problems for visitors in Canmore and Kananaskis that no existing platform fully solves.

Safety information is fragmented

Trail conditions, wildlife alerts and weather live on separate government websites — in English only, with no contextual connection to what a visitor is planning to do. International visitors routinely head out without knowing current conditions. SomaVio surfaces official information in the right language at the right moment.

Generic recommendations miss the moment

Review platforms and destination websites offer static lists. They don't know it's raining, that the trail is closed, or that a visitor just finished a 4-hour hike. Contextual recommendations — based on real conditions and visitor intent — are largely absent from the in-destination experience.

In-destination visitor behaviour is a data blind spot

Tourism organizations know how many visitors arrive and broad spending numbers. What they don't know is what visitors actually do once they're here — which trails, which categories, which contexts drive the most engagement. That gap makes it hard to optimize the visitor experience at a regional level.

How SomaVio works

01

Visitor accesses via QR or link

No app download. Visitors access SomaVio through a QR code at their hotel or a direct link — and immediately get a contextual guide for where they are and what's happening today.

02

Official safety always first

Safety information from Parks Canada, Alberta Parks and Environment Canada appears before any commercial content. Attribution is explicit. Sources are always visible. Language is calm and informative — never alarmist.

03

Contextual local recommendations

Recommendations adapt to conditions — weather, time of day, trail status. Hotels and local businesses can feature their offers within a contextual, curated framework. No paid-placement algorithm.

Regional data potential

As SomaVio scales, aggregated visitor behaviour signals could offer tourism organizations a new view of in-destination patterns. All data is anonymized. No individual visitor data is ever shared.

Category engagement by time of day

Understand which experience categories — outdoor, wellness, dining, cultural — are most engaged during different periods and weather conditions.

Illustrative: "Rainy afternoon wellness engagement is 3× higher than fair-weather days."

Visitor language and origin patterns

Aggregated language usage data can indicate visitor origin patterns and multilingual demand — informing translation priorities and market focus.

Illustrative: "PT and JA are the most active non-EN languages in Canmore in Q1."

Safety content engagement

Track how often safety information is accessed, which alert types generate the most engagement, and whether visitors are acting on official guidance.

Illustrative: "Wildlife alert views increased 40% on days with active Parks Canada notices."

Seasonal and temporal patterns

Understand how visitor intent and engagement patterns shift across seasons, times of week and weather conditions — useful for planning and capacity management.

Illustrative: "Indoor activity engagement peaks on weekends with afternoon precipitation."

Where we are now

Pilot validation phase

Early stage. Being honest about it.

SomaVio is in active development and early validation. We are not claiming endorsement from any government body or tourism organization. We are not making projections about scale or impact. What we are doing:

  • Building and testing the product with a small cohort of hotels and local businesses in Canmore
  • Validating that contextual recommendations and safety information improve visitor experience
  • Learning what data signals are meaningful and how to present them usefully
  • Identifying whether there is a genuine role for SomaVio in Alberta's wider tourism ecosystem
References to organizations, government bodies and tourism initiatives on this page do not imply partnership, endorsement, approval or participation. All data is cited with source. Illustrative scenarios are clearly labelled as such.
